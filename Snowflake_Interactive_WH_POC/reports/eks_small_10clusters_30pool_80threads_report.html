Test SQL Query
SELECT
    CR.CR_ORDER_NUMBER AS ORDER_KEY,
    CR.CR_REFUNDED_CUSTOMER_SK AS CUST_KEY,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CR.CR_REFUNDED_CUSTOMER_SK) OVER (PARTITION BY CR.CR_ORDER_NUMBER) AS ORDER_CUST_COUNT,
    SUM(COALESCE(CR.CR_RETURN_AMOUNT, 0)) AS TOTAL_RETURN_AMOUNT,
    MAX(CR.CR_REASON_SK) AS MAX_REASON,
    MAX(CR.CR_CALL_CENTER_SK) AS MAX_CALL_CENTER,
    MIN(CR.CR_RETURNED_DATE_SK) AS MIN_RETURN_DATE_SK,
    MAX(CR.CR_RETURNED_DATE_SK) AS MAX_RETURN_DATE_SK
FROM INTERACTIVE_DEMO.TPCH_INTERACTIVE.ORDERS_INTERACTIVE AS CR
WHERE CR.CR_RETURNED_DATE_SK BETWEEN 2452912 AND 2452924
  AND CR.CR_WAREHOUSE_SK IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY CR.CR_ORDER_NUMBER, CR.CR_REFUNDED_CUSTOMER_SK

Key Findings

  • SMALL warehouse delivers lower latency than XSMALL: P50 improved by 10ms (142 vs 152), P99 dramatically improved by 179ms (251 vs 430). The additional compute per cluster reduces tail latency significantly.
  • QPS limited by EKS pod count, not Snowflake: Only 12 of 15 pods ran due to VPC CNI IP allocation failures on EKS Auto Mode nodes. Zero Snowflake-side queuing confirms the warehouse had capacity for higher throughput.
  • Perfect cluster balance: All 10 clusters received 9.6% – 11.0% of queries, demonstrating excellent Interactive WH load distribution.
  • Estimated QPS at 15 pods: Extrapolating from 12 pods (418 QPS), 15 pods would yield ~523 QPS. Still below Kapil's 1,000 QPS due to reduced pod CPU (0.5 request vs 2) causing higher client-side overhead.
  • Near-zero errors: Only 1 timeout out of 730,000 queries (0.00014% failure rate) vs. Kapil's 961 warmup timeouts. The pre-warmed cache from the single-pod phase eliminated cold-start issues.
  • VPC CNI issue: EKS Auto Mode provisioned nodes that couldn't allocate secondary IPs via the aws-cni plugin. This is an infrastructure issue unrelated to Snowflake performance. Workaround: removed pod anti-affinity and lowered resource requests to co-locate pods on working nodes.
  • Single pod baseline: 1 pod with 30 connections sustains ~100 QPS with P50 ~131ms, providing a useful per-pod throughput reference.
