Test Query
SELECT
    CR.CR_ORDER_NUMBER AS ORDER_KEY,
    CR.CR_REFUNDED_CUSTOMER_SK AS CUST_KEY,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CR.CR_REFUNDED_CUSTOMER_SK) OVER (PARTITION BY CR.CR_ORDER_NUMBER) AS ORDER_CUST_COUNT,
    SUM(COALESCE(CR.CR_RETURN_AMOUNT, 0)) AS TOTAL_RETURN_AMOUNT,
    MAX(CR.CR_REASON_SK) AS MAX_REASON,
    MAX(CR.CR_CALL_CENTER_SK) AS MAX_CALL_CENTER,
    MIN(CR.CR_RETURNED_DATE_SK) AS MIN_RETURN_DATE_SK,
    MAX(CR.CR_RETURNED_DATE_SK) AS MAX_RETURN_DATE_SK
FROM INTERACTIVE_DEMO.TPCH_INTERACTIVE.ORDERS_INTERACTIVE AS CR
WHERE CR.CR_RETURNED_DATE_SK BETWEEN 2452912 AND 2452924
  AND CR.CR_WAREHOUSE_SK IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY CR.CR_ORDER_NUMBER, CR.CR_REFUNDED_CUSTOMER_SK

Infrastructure Notes

EKS Deployment Details

  • EKS Cluster: rnellikal-jmeter-interactive-poc (us-west-2, Auto Mode, K8s 1.35)
  • 15 pods deployed across 4 working nodes (7+6+1+1 distribution)
  • 10 nodes cordoned due to VPC CNI IP exhaustion (/24 subnet limitation)
  • Pods colocated without anti-affinity to work around CNI issue
  • Docker image: 087354435437.dkr.ecr.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/jmeter-snowflake:latest
  • JMeter 5.6.3 + Snowflake JDBC 3.20.0 + Eclipse Temurin JRE 21
  • JVM: 2GB heap, Arrow format enabled, connection keep-alive

Analysis & Observations

  • 33% QPS improvement over previous test (418 → 555 QPS) with all 15 pods active vs 12
  • All 10 warehouse clusters active and evenly loaded (9.5-10.6% each)
  • Zero queue overload — warehouse not saturated, headroom available
  • P50 latency (164ms) comparable to Kapil's XSMALL baseline (152ms)
  • QPS gap vs Kapil (555 vs 1,001): Different account/warehouse/region may explain difference
  • S3 "SlowDown" throttling observed — may cap effective throughput at client side
  • Two-phase latency pattern: earlier minutes (18:49-19:01) show P99 ~350-400ms; later minutes (19:02+) show P99 ~470-520ms, possibly due to S3 throttling accumulation
